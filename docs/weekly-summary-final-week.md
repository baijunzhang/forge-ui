## 2. Desk shadowing — learnings and AI Markets opportunities

| Desk | Key learning | Potential AI Markets opportunity |

| **STIR** | Short-term rates linked to FX swaps, IRS, CCS, funding conditions and central-bank policy; manages client flow + rate/basis/liquidity/funding risk; voice vs electronic by size/liquidity/complexity | Monitor central-bank & funding developments, summarise curve/basis moves, support pre-trade analysis, explain pricing drivers faster (human confirmation & clear sources remain key) |

## 3. Rotation reflection

My biggest takeaway is a much clearer understanding of what it means to build a **fintech platform**. A strong platform is not a collection of isolated AI features — it needs to connect data, systems and specialist capabilities, understand the user's intent, coordinate the right tools, and return an output that is reliable, transparent and easy to use. Every desk has different products, data, risks and pain points, so the most valuable AI use cases are the ones that connect directly into a desk's existing daily workflow.

This rotation also showed me why **both technology and business knowledge are essential**: technology alone can produce features that don't solve a real problem, while business knowledge without knowing what technology enables can limit the solution. The Bloomberg connectivity project and Japan Wealth Sales parser testing both showed how product development, prompting, execution and testing connect — and reinforced that **trust** is especially important in financial applications: users need clear sources, reliable outputs, appropriate human oversight, and confidence that the system understands the product and business context.
